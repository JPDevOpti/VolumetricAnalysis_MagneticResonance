Analysis of Cortical Curvature Differences

The analysis of cortical curvature revealed significant differences between groups in four specific regions:

  • Left Isthmus Cingulate: The GW group showed higher curvature (0.1230) compared to the GK group (0.1170), with a p-value of 0.0370. This difference was assessed using the Mann-Whitney U test due to non-normal distribution in both groups.
  • Left Pars Triangularis: The GK group exhibited slightly higher curvature (0.1210) than the GW group (0.1180), with a p-value of 0.0341. This difference was analyzed using Welch's t-test.
  • Right Isthmus Cingulate: The GW group showed higher curvature (0.1235) compared to the GK group (0.1170), with a highly significant p-value of 0.0033. This was the most significant difference found in the curvature analysis.
  • Right Temporal Pole: The GW group demonstrated higher curvature (0.1460) than the GK group (0.1370), with a p-value of 0.0243.

These findings suggest that there are distinct patterns of cortical folding in these specific regions between the two groups. The most notable differences were observed in the isthmus cingulate regions bilaterally, with the right hemisphere showing the strongest effect. The temporal pole and pars triangularis also showed significant differences, indicating potential variations in cortical development or organization in these areas.

Analysis of Cortical Folding Index Differences

The analysis of cortical folding index revealed significant differences between groups in three specific regions:

  • Left Frontal Pole: The GK group showed higher folding index (6.0) compared to the GW group (5.0), with a p-value of 0.0402. This difference was assessed using the Mann-Whitney U test due to non-normal distribution in both groups.
  • Right Parahippocampal: The GK group exhibited higher folding index (6.0) than the GW group (5.0), with a p-value of 0.0270. This difference was also analyzed using the Mann-Whitney U test due to non-normal distribution.
  • Right Temporal Pole: The GW group demonstrated higher folding index (8.5) compared to the GK group (8.0), with a p-value of 0.0155. This was the most significant difference found in the folding index analysis and was assessed using Welch's t-test.

These findings indicate distinct patterns of cortical folding complexity in these specific regions between the two groups. The most notable differences were observed in the temporal pole, which showed the strongest effect. The frontal pole and parahippocampal regions also showed significant differences, suggesting variations in cortical development or organization in these areas. The higher folding indices in the GK group for the frontal pole and parahippocampal regions, contrasted with the higher index in the GW group for the temporal pole, may reflect different patterns of cortical organization between the groups.
